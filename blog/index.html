Spreadsheet workflow

How to use a Sugargoo spreadsheet without wasting product tabs.

Start from intent instead of opening everything. Pick the missing outfit role, scan only that category, then compare silhouette, fabric, QC risk, and whether the item gives the haul a clear job.

QC photos

The buyer checks that matter before shipping.

QC is not a logo hunt. Review the full shape first, then material, stitching, wash, zipper lines, pocket placement, outsole height, and color under neutral light.

Category notes

Streetwear categories that make a haul easier to build.

Sneakers anchor proportion, hoodies add repeat wear, jackets shape the whole silhouette, and accessories finish a simple fit. Browse by role, not by random rows.

Sneaker finds

Why sneaker spreadsheet rows need stricter QC.

Toe box height, heel curve, side profile, panel alignment, and sole thickness change how a pair wears. A popular colorway still fails if the shape looks wrong in profile.

Hoodies and layers

How to judge basics before they become dead weight.

Good hoodies and jackets should improve a basic rotation. Check fabric weight, sleeve drop, ribbing, collar shape, cuff strength, and whether the piece layers over what you already own.

Parcel planning

Prepare a haul before thinking about shipping speed.

A cleaner parcel starts before checkout: remove weak finds, group items by season and weight, confirm QC issues early, and avoid adding filler pieces only because the spreadsheet made them easy to click.
